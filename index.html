About Me

Hello, I am Albert Einstein and I was born at Ulm, in Wurttemberg, Germany, on March 14, 1879. I was grew up in Munich, where my father founded an electrical engineering company. After studying at the ETH university in Zurich, I worked at the patent office in Bern, during which time I produced several pioneering works in the field of physics.

I am a German-born theoretical physicist who is widely held as one of the most influential scientists. I am Best known for developing the theory of relativity, I also made important contributions to quantum mechanics. My mass-energy equivalence formula E = mc^2, which arises from special relativity, has been called "the world's most famous equation". I received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics "for my services to theoretical physics, and especially for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect", a pivotal step in the development of quantum theory. I received the 1921 Nobel Prize in Physics. My Famous Quotations are: We cannot solve our problems with the same thinking we used when we created them. "Learn from yesterday, live for today, hope for tomorrow. The important thing is not to stop questioning."

The History of Black Holes

Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so intense that nothing can escape, not even light. The boundary where the curvature of spacetime inevitably leads into the black hole is known as the event horizon.

Time In Physics

In classical physics, time was viewed as an absolute and universal quantity, flowing at a constant rate independent of external factors. This notion, proposed by Sir Isaac Newton, treated time as separate from space, forming the backdrop against which physical events unfold.
